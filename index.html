Ramya Hebbalaguppe

Reliable Machine Learning · Confidence Calibration · OOD Detection · Vision-Language Models · Generative AI

I am a Senior Scientist at TCS Research, working in the Deep Learning and Artificial Intelligence Group at the Research and Innovation Park, IIT Delhi. My doctoral research, supervised by Prof. Chetan Arora at IIT Delhi, focuses on improving the reliability of deep neural network classifiers. My work studies confidence calibration, out-of-distribution detection, uncertainty quantification, refinement, knowledge distillation, and test-time adaptation for DNNs and vision-language models.

A central theme of my research is that accuracy alone is not sufficient for deployment in safety-critical settings. I develop methods that make models not only accurate, but also aware of their uncertainty, robust under distribution shift, and better calibrated for real-world decision making.

Before my doctoral research, I worked with Prof. Ramakrishna Kakarala at Nanyang Technological University on High Dynamic Range imaging algorithms for smartphone camera pipelines. This work received the Best Student Paper Award at SPIE 2012. I completed my Master’s degree at Dublin City University in 2014, advised by Prof. Noel O'Connor and Prof. Alan Smeaton, where I worked on reducing false alarms in surveillance camera networks. Part of this work was licensed to Netwatch Systems, and the team received the Invent Award.

At TCS, my broader research interests include efficient inference through model compression, trustworthy ML, continual learning, and creative/immersive AI for images, videos, and 3D/4D content generation. Outside work, I enjoy painting, traveling, cooking, baking, composting, planting trees, and music.

Thesis Overview

My doctoral research develops a unified view of reliability in DNN classifiers: accuracy → calibration → OOD detection → refinement → lightweight reliability → test-time calibration. Rather than treating uncertainty as a post-hoc correction, this line of work studies reliability as a first-class design principle during training, distillation, representation learning, and test-time adaptation.

Selected Publications

Representative papers spanning reliable machine learning, calibration, out-of-distribution detection, efficient models, and 2D/3D/4D computer vision are highlighted below.

Refine and Align: Confidence Calibration Through Multi-Agent Interaction in VQA
Ayush Pandey, Jai Bardhan, Ishita Jain, Ramya Hebbalaguppe, Rohan Raju Dhanakshirur, Lovekesh Vig
AAAI 2026 (Alignment Track) AAAI 2026

We study confidence calibration in visual question answering, where overconfident predictions can be risky in high-stakes applications. AlignVQA uses a debate-based multi-agent framework, together with an AlignCal training loss, to improve confidence estimates across VQA benchmarks.

Calibration Transfer via Knowledge Distillation
Ramya Hebbalaguppe, Mayank Baranwal, Kartik Anand, Chetan Arora
ACCV 2024 Oral · Top 5.6%
[paper] [supplement]

KD(C) extends knowledge distillation beyond accuracy transfer. We show that a calibrated teacher can produce lightweight student models that are both accurate and well-calibrated, and in some cases better calibrated than the teacher.

Lifelong Learning in StyleGAN through Latent Subspaces
Adarsh K, Anmol Garg, Ramya Hebbalaguppe, Prathosh AP
Transactions on Machine Learning Research, 2024

StyleCL enables lifelong learning in StyleGAN by learning task-specific latent subspace dictionaries and lightweight feature adaptors. It reduces catastrophic forgetting while requiring significantly fewer additional parameters per task.

LoMOE: Localized Multi-Object Editing via Multi-Diffusion
Goirik Chakrabarty, Aditya Chandrasekar, Ramya Hebbalaguppe, Prathosh AP
ACM International Conference on Multimedia, 2024
[paper] [project page]

LoMOE is a zero-shot multi-diffusion framework for localized multi-object editing. It supports additions, replacements, and fine-grained edits in a single pass, and introduces LoMOE-Bench for evaluating localized editing quality.

ReMOVE: A Reference-free Metric for Object Erasure
Aditya Chandrasekar, Goirik Chakrabarty, Jai Bardhan, Ramya Hebbalaguppe, Prathosh AP
CVPR Workshop on the Evaluation of Generative Foundation Models, 2024
[project page]

ReMOVE is a reference-free metric for evaluating object erasure in diffusion-based image editing. It addresses settings where reference images are unavailable and helps distinguish true object removal from replacement artifacts.

Transfer4D: A Framework for Frugal Motion Capture and Deformation Transfer
Shubh Maheshwari, Rahul Narain, Ramya Hebbalaguppe
IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition, 2023
[project page]

Transfer4D enables frugal motion capture and deformation transfer using commodity depth sensors. It transfers motion from incomplete single-view depth video to semantically similar target meshes, reducing dependence on expensive motion-capture setups.

Calibrating Deep Neural Networks Using Explicit Regularisation and Dynamic Data Pruning
Rishabh Patra*, Ramya Hebbalaguppe*, Tirtharaj Dash, Gautam Shroff, Lovekesh Vig
IEEE/CVF Winter Conference on Applications of Computer Vision, 2023 Spotlight · Top 10%
[project page]

We propose an explicit differentiable calibration regularizer and a dynamic data pruning strategy. The approach improves calibration while reducing training effort by focusing optimization on useful high-confidence samples.

A Novel Data Augmentation Technique for Out-of-Distribution Sample Detection using Compounded Corruptions
Ramya Hebbalaguppe, Soumya Suvra Ghosal, Jatin Prakash, Harshad Khadilkar, Chetan Arora
European Conference on Machine Learning, 2022
[project page]

CnC synthesizes out-of-distribution samples through compounded corruptions without requiring external hold-out data. The resulting model improves OOD detection accuracy and inference efficiency against a wide range of baselines.

A Stitch in Time Saves Nine: A Train-Time Regularizing Loss for Improved Neural Network Calibration
Ramya Hebbalaguppe*, Jatin Prakash, Neelabh Madan*, Chetan Arora
IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition, 2022 Oral · Top 4%
[project page]

We introduce Multi-class Difference in Confidence and Accuracy (MDCA), a train-time auxiliary loss for neural network calibration. MDCA can be combined with standard task losses and extends across image classification, NLP, speech, and semantic segmentation settings.
